# Refund Policy & Retention Playbook

We aim to keep our customers satisfied, but we maintain structured rules regarding payments, refunds, and customer retention.

## General Refund Rules
- **14-Day Money-Back Guarantee**: New customers can request a **full refund within 14 days** of their initial purchase if they are unsatisfied with the product.
- **No Refunds After 14 Days**: After the initial 14 days, all fees are non-refundable. This applies to standard monthly subscriptions, annual plans, and seat additions.
- **Auto-Renewals**: Standard monthly renewals are non-refundable unless requested within 48 hours of the charge and showing zero platform usage during that period.

## Exception Process
Exceptions to the 14-day rule can only be approved by a Customer Success Manager or Billing Director under the following conditions:
1. Extended service outages (P0) exceeding 4 consecutive hours where no alternative was provided.
2. Technical system bugs preventing core platform operations that remained unresolved for over 7 business days.
3. Chatbot or sales agent misinformation regarding critical capabilities (requires audit proof of the misinformation).

## Credits vs Refunds
Whenever possible, support agents should offer **Service Credits** instead of direct cash refunds:
- Service credits apply directly to the next billing cycle and preserve cash flow.
- A maximum of 1 month of service credit can be granted by senior support agents. Higher credit amounts require manager approval.

## Churn Retention Playbook
When a customer threatens to churn or leave negative reviews due to pricing or service issues, follow this retention flow:
1. **Acknowledge and Validate**: Apologize for the friction and validate their frustration (especially if there was a delay in response).
2. **First Line of Retention (Discounts)**: Offer a temporary **20% discount for the next 3 months** to ease their financial commitment.
3. **Second Line of Retention (Service Credit)**: If the churn threat is due to a bug or outage, offer up to **1 month of service credit** as a goodwill gesture.
4. **Third Line of Retention (Custom Plan)**: Downsize them to a lower tier (e.g., from Pro to Standard) rather than letting them cancel completely, ensuring they maintain access to their historical data.
